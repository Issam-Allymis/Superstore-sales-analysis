# Superstore Sales Analysis

## Overview
This project analyses retail sales data to indetify key drivers of revenue and profitability. The goal is to uncover insights that can help improve business perfomance and decision making.

## Objective 
To analyse sales, profit, and discout data in order to:
- Identify top-performing categories
- Detect areas of low profitability
- Understand the impact of discounts on profit

---

## Tools & technologies
- Pandas
- Python
- Matplotlb
- Kaggle Dataset
- Google Colab

---

## Dataset
The dataset used is the **Superstore dataset**, which contains information on:
- Sales
- Profit
- Discounts
- Product Categories

---

## Key Analysis
### 1. Category Performance
- Analysed total sales and profit by category
  **Finding:**
  Technology generates the highest sales and profit, while Furniture shows high sales but significantly lower profit.

---

### 2. Discount vs Profit
- Examined correlation between discount level and profit
- Analysed average profit at different discount levels
**FindingL**
  Higher discounts are associated with lower profitability. Discounts above ~30 lead to consistent losses.

---

### 3. Root Cause Analysis
- Compared average discount by category

**Finding:**
Furniture has the highest average discount, explaining its low profitability despite strong sales.

---

## Key Insights
- Technology is the most profitable category
- Furniture underperforms due to excessive discounts
- High discount levels (30%+) negatively impact profit
- Optimising discount strategy could significantly improve business performance

---

## Conclusion
The analysis shows that while some categories drive strong revenue, excessive discounts particularly in furniture is reducing overall profitability. 
Reducing discount levels could lead to improve financial performance.
